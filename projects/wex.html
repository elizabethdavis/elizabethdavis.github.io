    My Journey at WEX: 5+ Years of Growth and Innovation in Product Design

    Introduction

    Since joining WEX in 2019, my career has been an exciting journey of growth, innovation, and impactful contributions in product design. Specializing in benefits software, I've had the privilege of shaping digital experiences that make a real difference in people's lives. It's really challenging to describe this experience as just a handful of projects on my portfolio, because it has been so much more than that. I hope this offers a glimpse into my professional evolution, key achievements, and the valuable lessons I've learned along the way.


    Career Progression

    2019: Embarking on the WEX Journey

    I started at WEX as an Individual Contributor, eager to apply my UX skills to the complex world of benefits software. From day one, I was immersed in challenging projects that pushed me to grow both creatively and technically.

    2020: Rising to Leadership

    After just a year and a half, my dedication and innovative approach to UX design were recognized with a promotion to a leadership position. This new role allowed me to not only continue hands-on design work but also to guide and inspire others.

    2022: Growing My Team

    After increased demand for UX design partnership across the organization, our team has continued to grow. Through a company reorg, we restructured our design team to help manage the workload. As a result, my team grew to three direct reports within Benefits.

    Today: Leading a Dynamic Design Team

    Presently, I manage a team of 5 talented UX professionals. Together, we tackle 20-25 efforts annually ranging from large scale redesigns to smaller efforts, driving innovation and user-centric design in benefits software solutions. Our work impacts millions of users, simplifying complex processes and enhancing the overall experience of managing benefits.


    Key Achievements

    Throughout my tenure at WEX, I've had the opportunity to lead several transformative initiatives. Here are four key projects that showcase the breadth and impact of our work:

    Reimagining the Benefits Enrollment Experience

    Challenge: High drop-off rates and user confusion during the benefits enrollment process.
    Solution: Led a complete redesign of the enrollment flow, incorporating user feedback and behavioral insights.
    Outcome: Achieved a 30% reduction in user errors and a 25% increase in completion rates.
    Impact: Simplified a crucial annual process for thousands of employees across multiple client companies.

    Mobile-First Health Savings Account (HSA) Management

    Challenge: Limited mobile access to HSA information and transactions.
    Solution: Spearheaded the development of a mobile-first HSA management app.
    Outcome: Increased mobile engagement by 150% and improved user satisfaction scores by 40%.
    Impact: Empowered users to make informed health spending decisions on-the-go.

    AI-Powered Benefits Recommendation Engine

    Challenge: Users struggling to choose the most appropriate benefits package.
    Solution: Designed an AI-driven recommendation system that analyzes user data and preferences.
    Outcome: 85% of users reported feeling more confident in their benefits choices.
    Impact: Personalized the benefits selection process, leading to better coverage and financial outcomes for employees.

    Accessibility Overhaul Initiative

    Challenge: Ensuring all our digital products meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards.
    Solution: Led a company-wide initiative to audit, redesign, and implement accessibility improvements across all platforms.
    Outcome: Achieved WCAG 2.1 AA compliance across 95% of our digital products.
    Impact: Made benefits management more inclusive and accessible to all users, regardless of abilities.

    Scope of Work and Team Management

    Managing a team that handles 20-25 major projects annually requires a delicate balance of strategic thinking, hands-on design work, and effective leadership. Here's a glimpse into how we operate:

  • Agile Methodology: We employ a modified agile approach, allowing us to be responsive to changing user needs and technological advancements.
  • Cross-Functional Collaboration: Regular collaboration with product managers, developers, and business stakeholders ensures alignment and efficient execution.
  • Continuous Learning: We dedicate time each sprint for learning and experimentation, keeping our skills sharp and our solutions innovative.
  • User-Centric Focus: Every project begins and ends with user research, ensuring we're always solving real problems for real people.

    • Personal Growth and Lessons Learned

    My years at WEX have been transformative, both professionally and personally. Here are some key lessons and areas of growth:

  • Balancing Vision and Execution: Learning to think strategically while still being able to roll up my sleeves and contribute to hands-on design work.
  • The Power of Empathy: Deepening my understanding of user needs and how to translate those into effective design solutions.
  • Leadership Through Trust: Developing a leadership style that empowers team members to take ownership and innovate.
  • Navigating Complexity: Honing the ability to simplify complex systems and processes for end-users without losing necessary functionality.
  • Data-Driven Design: Strengthening my skills in using data and analytics to inform design decisions and measure impact.
